MUSIC DIVERSITY ACROSS DECADES

A Legacy
of Genres

As we journey through the decades, we see how different types of music emerged and evolved. Jazz filled the speakeasies of the 1920s, rock exploded in the 1960s, and the 2000s and 2010s brought a diversity with countless new genres. Each era left its mark, and together they tell the story of how music production and diversity changed over time.

Tracks Genres

As technology advanced, allowing music to be shared among wider groups of people, more music was produced. The visualization shows this dramatic growth from the early decades through to the 2010s, reflecting how recording technology and distribution methods democratized music creation.

As production increased, so did genres, which became richer over time as new genres emerged from the combination of older ones.

Music is a legacy that is passed onto us by the last generation. Each generation’s music built upon the last and further enhanced it with the taste of the current cultural movement. This happens alongside other pop culture like fashion and films, creating a continuous stream where music from one decade influences the next and connects people across all eras

TRACKS AND GENRES EVOLUTION

A Journey
Through Sound

The sound of music itself has transformed over time. What started as acoustic recordings gradually gave way to electronic production. Music became louder and more energetic, and the emotional tone shifted with each decade. Through danceability, energy, acousticness, and other characteristics, we can hear how the very nature of music changed across the century.

ARTIST NETWORKS AND CONNECTIONS

A Lens
Into Collaboration

Artists work by mixing different sounds into rhythms to create the music we hear. In earlier decades, artists often worked alone or within small local groups. But as digital technology emerged, these networks grew bigger. Artists began connecting across different styles, different eras, and different places.
