Four-part structure (Asana framework)

1
Problem & context (~80 words)

UrbanMove's Mobility-as-a-Service rollout for Queensland councils has surfaced four interconnected professional problems: inconsistent platform reliability across council deployments, a breakdown in communication and governance across stakeholders, Agile team coordination failures amid rapid expansion, and a cybersecurity/privacy incident involving the potential re-identification of commuter trip data. Left unaddressed, these issues put contractual obligations, council reputations, and public trust at significant risk.

2
Key findings — 4 issues (~150 words)

Quality: Inconsistent behaviour across councils, a growing defect/change backlog, and undefined third-party integration standards point to gaps in the quality framework (ISO/IEC 25010).
Communication: Misalignment on responsibilities and SLAs — compounded by the Silicon Valley provider's API rigidity and the Brisbane startup's capacity constraints — shows a failure of shared communication scaffolding.
Teams: Multiple parallel Agile squads without coordination structures, contractors and offshore developers with unclear onboarding, and intercultural gaps have created knowledge silos and divergent priorities.
Ethics/Privacy: The cybersecurity incident exposed "de-identified" trip data that can be re-identified via cross-referencing public data — a breach of Australian Privacy Principles and a serious reputational risk for councils.

3
Recommendations (~120 words)

I recommend: (1) adopting an ISO/IEC 25010-aligned quality assurance framework with mandatory integration testing; (2) establishing a cross-stakeholder RACI matrix and a change governance protocol; (3) restructuring parallel Agile delivery using a Spotify-inspired squad/tribe model with integration liaison roles; and (4) commissioning an immediate Privacy Impact Assessment and upgrading de-identification methodology to align with the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs) and Notifiable Data Breaches scheme obligations.

4
Conclusion (~80 words)

Resolving these four issues is not optional — UrbanMove's ability to meet its contractual obligations, retain council partners, and scale responsibly depends on it. The recommendations in this report provide a structured, prioritised path to restore stakeholder confidence, protect public trust, and deliver a platform that councils can stand behind. This report details the specific actions, frameworks, and accountabilities required to achieve that outcome.

4
Recommendations
One per issue — actionable, prescriptive, first-person
~500 words ▾
Business reports are prescriptive — write "I recommend…", not "it would be beneficial to consider…". Each rec should be concrete and actionable.
R1
Implement an ISO/IEC 25010-aligned quality assurance framework

Define quality attributes (functional suitability, reliability, portability, security) per council deployment. Mandate integration testing protocols for every third-party API before go-live. Introduce a defect priority matrix with resolution SLAs. Schedule quarterly quality audits across all council deployments.

R2
Establish a cross-stakeholder RACI matrix and change governance framework

Define who is Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, and Informed for every integration and decision point across UrbanMove, councils, and third-party providers. Introduce a change notification protocol requiring cross-stakeholder sign-off before any deployment. Hold regular joint steering meetings. Tailor communication style per provider (structured/formal for Silicon Valley; supportive/collaborative for Brisbane startup).

R3
Restructure Agile delivery using a Spotify-inspired squad/tribe model

Establish squads with clear ownership, a tribe layer for cross-squad coordination, and chapter leads for shared technical standards. Assign dedicated integration liaison roles between UrbanMove and each third-party provider. Introduce an intercultural awareness onboarding program for offshore and contractor team members. Run shared backlog reviews to surface cross-squad dependencies early.

R4
Commission a Privacy Impact Assessment and remediate data governance

Engage legal counsel immediately to assess APP obligations and Notifiable Data Breach scheme exposure. Upgrade de-identification methodology (consider k-anonymity or differential privacy to address the mosaic effect). Publish a plain-language data transparency statement for commuters (APP 1). Conduct a third-party API security audit and tighten access controls. Report findings to affected councils promptly.
